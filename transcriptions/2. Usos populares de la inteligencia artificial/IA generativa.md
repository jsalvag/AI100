- Over a thousand years ago,
alchemy was one of the most popular branches of philosophy.
It was the science of trying to take a combination
of everyday things and turn it into something valuable.
One of the most popular stories in alchemy
was the philosopher's stone.
The philosopher's stone could transform worthless metal,
like lead into silver or gold.
It was even rumored to be able to turn water
into a substance that would bring people back to life.
So, there's a long tradition of taking something
that's plentiful and turning it into something valuable.
Fast forward to today, and you have generative AI.
Generative AI is about taking massive amounts of data
and converting it into something valuable.
The idea behind it is that if you can get an AI system
to vacuum up all the world's data,
then it can generate something new.
Remember that predictive AI is about using
a smaller amount of data to make accurate predictions.
So, you might be able to guess what music a customer likes,
and you can guess what items they'll buy.
But each system has one task.
Your music prediction and your purchase prediction
will be two different systems.
Generative AI is about training a system
on huge amounts of data
so it can perform many different tasks.
It can predict your purchases, understand your music tastes,
and even generate video.
In the words of JRR Tolkien,
it's "One ring to rule them all..."
Just like the alchemists from thousands of years ago,
if the system can gather up enough everyday data,
it can create something much more precious.
So, think of it this way.
Imagine that you wanted to create a banking chatbot
that answered common questions.
You could create a predictive AI system
that could quickly answer very specific questions.
It could answer, "How do I make this transaction?"
Or, "What's the balance on this account?"
In fact, you may have seen a simple chatbot
like this already on your bank's website.
On the other hand, you could use a generative AI system.
The AI system would generate
much more information from the chat,
but this system would need much more data
and processing power.
This new Gen AI system has the capability
to address a wider range of general questions.
It could answer a question like,
"Will today's financial news
affect my balance in my account?"
Or, "Is this transaction tax-deductible?"
To answer these questions, you'd need a system
that has a lot of information and can make connections.
These connections require a lot of data.
So, if you're using the system for your organization,
you need to balance the two possibilities.
Would you want a sleek system
that doesn't require much processing power,
but has a lot less flexibility?
Or do you prefer a more complex system
that needs a lot of data but can answer more questions?
One is small and specific,
and the other is large and more flexible.
A generative AI system can typically do
almost everything that a predictive system can do,
but not the other way around.
