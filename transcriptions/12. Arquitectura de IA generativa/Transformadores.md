- In 2017, researchers at Google
and the University of Toronto
came up with a paper called "Attention is All You Need."
This paper was trying to come up
with a new way to translate English into French and German.
There are a lot of sentences
where a simple change in word order
can dramatically change the meaning.
Think about these two sentences.
I want to paint the wall blue.
I want to paint the blue wall.
In the first sentence,
you have no idea what color the wall is you want to paint.
It could be black, brown, or even unpainted.
In the second sentence, you know the color of the wall,
but you have no idea what color to paint the wall.
That means that when you translate these two sentences,
you need to really understand
the differences between the two meanings.
To solve this problem,
these researchers proposed a transformer model.
This model is like the autoencoders that you saw earlier.
Remember that autoencoders
find the essence of the item in the photograph.
You could almost think of it
like a pencil drawing.
The system encodes the tree and then uses that code
to decode images of other trees.
A transformer uses a similar technique
to encode large blocks of text,
except here, the system is trying to find
the purpose of the sentence
instead of the essence of the image.
It does this by assigning a numerical value
to how each word is ordered in the sentence.
Then over time, the system can start
to learn the relationship
between the purpose and when the word appears in a sentence.
Now, when the researchers came up with this model
back in 2017, they were focused on language translation,
but it turns out that transformers
were one of the best ways to understand
the purpose behind language.
That's why in 2019,
companies such as Google and OpenAI started
to create transformer-based chat systems.
Remember that ChatGPT stands
for generative pre-trained transformer.
One key thing to remember
is that transformer-based models are all about attention.
It's about helping the model better understand context
so it can generate something that makes sense.
This attention is not only used for text,
but now can increasingly be used for images as well.
It can help image generators find faces
or key parts of an image.
Another interesting innovation
has been the rise of what's called self attention.
That at first might sound like self care.
You can almost imagine an AI transformer
taking a yoga break, but a self-attention transformer
can go through terabytes
or even petabytes of data and understand
the relationship between a word order
and the purpose of the sentence.
It can do this with very little human intervention.
The system can generate words
in a sentence or parts of an image
based on a much larger context.
That means that the system can generate a sentence like,
I want to paint the blue wall
in front of the house and also generate,
I want to paint the wall blue
because I don't like the color black.
The system will successfully generate the second
part of the sentence because it understands the first part.
It turns out that it's much easier for AI systems
to understand human language by paying very close attention.
